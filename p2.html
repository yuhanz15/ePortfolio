Modeling Tennis Momentum Shifts

This project was carried out as part of the 2024 Mathematical Contest in Modeling (MCM). I worked in a group of two and was primarily the one to create the initial momentum flow model, and was involved in creating predictive models for the analysis and prediction of alterations in player performance during tennis matches.

We initially constructed a mathematical model to quantify momentum in a match from performance indicators such as point winnings, aces, double faults, and serving. After we had enhanced the model to better approximate true patterns of match play, we demonstrated that tennis momentum is structured and not random.

Using the momentum model, logistic and linear regression models for predicting point outcomes and variation in flow were developed. The models uncovered critical indicators such as server health and win-game ratio and utilized for examining predictability of change in momentum in and between games.

This project deepened my experience in time series modeling, sports analytics, and collaborative model development under time constraints.
