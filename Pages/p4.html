Medical Cost Modeling and Group-Based Prediction

This project was completed as part of the coursework for STAT 423 at the University of Washington. Working with teammates, I contributed mainly to the model refinement and subgroup analysis portions of the project.

The goal of this project was to build a predictive model for individual medical charges based on factors such as age, BMI, smoking status, and number of children. Using the Medical Cost Personal Dataset, we first conducted exploratory data analysis, assessed correlations, and built initial linear regression models.

Focusing on improving model performance, I led the effort to refine the predictors, identify groupings based on BMI and smoking status, and fit an updated model that significantly improved the explained variance. Our final model separated patients into three groups and achieved an adjusted R-squared of over 0.86.

Through this project, I gained deeper experience in model selection, group-based modeling strategies, and interpreting residual patterns to guide model improvements.
