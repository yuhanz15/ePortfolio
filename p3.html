Comparative Analysis of SARIMA and ETS Models in Forecasting Influenza Cases

This project was completed during the "Online Research Seminar on Time Series Modeling with Application in Economics and Public Health", held in 2024. It focuses on comparing the performance of two time series models—SARIMA and ETS—in forecasting monthly influenza cases in China.

Using China Public Health Science Data Center, 2012-2018 data, I formulated and contrasted the two models with regards to their ability to capture seasonality and predictive power. After fitting and testing on out-of-sample data, ETS yielded lower RMSE and MAPE values than SARIMA, which signifies enhanced forecasting accuracy.

From this project, I gained further insight into time series modeling, model performance, and the influence of model structures on forecast outcomes, especially when working with public health data.
